## How to Use This Guide

Each palette includes:
- **Role** — Where this color is used in the UI
- **Color Name** — Descriptive name for quick reference
- **HEX** — Standard web hex code
- **RGB** — Red, Green, Blue values
- **HSL** — Hue, Saturation, Lightness values
- **Usage Note** — Practical guidance on applying it

**Color Role Definitions:**

| Role          | Description                                                              |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Brand Primary | Main brand color — used in logo, headers, key UI elements                |
| Brand Secondary | Supporting brand color — used in sub-headers, icons, hover states       |
| Accent        | High-contrast pop color — used sparingly for badges, labels, highlights  |
| Background    | Page/section backgrounds                                                 |
| Surface       | Card backgrounds, panels, modals                                         |
| Text Primary  | Main body text                                                           |
| Text Secondary| Sub-text, captions, placeholders                                         |
| CTA / Button  | Call-to-action buttons — "Buy Now", "Add to Cart", "Checkout"            |
| Success       | Confirmations, availability, trust signals                               |
| Warning       | Low stock alerts, cautions                                               |
| Error         | Errors, out-of-stock, destructive actions                                |
| Border        | Dividers, outlines, card strokes                                         |

### The Science Behind CTA Button Colors

Research shows button colors significantly affect conversion:

- **Orange buttons** outperform green by ~12.5% in A/B tests (HubSpot, 2023)
- **Red buttons** generate highest urgency click-throughs but must be paired carefully
- **Contrast** matters more than hue — a purple button on a purple page converts poorly
- **Isolation effect** — surround your CTA with neutral space to maximize its click rate

## Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) Tips

### 1. The 60–30–10 Rule
- **60%** of your store should be your dominant background/neutral color
- **30%** should be your secondary/brand color
- **10%** should be your accent/CTA color — used ONLY on key actions

### 2. Trust Signals Need Blue or Green
- Payment badges, SSL indicators, and reviews perform best in blue/green tones
- Avoid using red near checkout — it signals danger/stop

### 3. Sale vs. Premium Pricing
- **Sale stores** use Red, Orange, Yellow → triggers urgency and affordability perception
- **Premium stores** use Black, Gold, Navy → triggers exclusivity and quality perception
- Never mix both strategies on the same product page

### 4. Product Photography Backgrounds
- **White background** = highest trust, best for search/marketplace listing pages
- **Lifestyle background** = best for hero sections and emotional storytelling
- **Colored background** = use your brand accent for featured/hero products only

### 5. Hover & Interaction Micro-Colors
- On hover, darken buttons by **10–15%** (not color-shift — darkening feels more natural)
- Use a subtle box-shadow on card hover: `box-shadow: 0 4px 20px rgba(0,0,0,0.12)`
- Avoid red hover states on non-destructive actions — it implies danger

### 6. Color and Gender Perception (Research Data)
- Women favor: Blue, Purple, Green | Dislike: Orange, Brown, Gray
- Men favor: Blue, Green, Black | Dislike: Purple, Orange, Brown
- Blue is universally the most trusted color across all demographics

### 7. Loading States & Skeleton Screens
- Use `#E2E8F0` (light gray) for skeleton/loading states — not pure white
- Subtle shimmer animation prevents bounce during load

## How to Build Your Own Palette

Follow this 5-step process to create a custom palette from scratch:

### Step 1 — Define Your Brand Personality
Answer these questions:
- Is your brand **serious or playful**?
- Is your price point **budget, mid-range, or luxury**?
- Is your audience **young or mature**? **Male, female, or neutral**?
- What **emotion** should customers feel when they see your store?

### Step 2 — Choose Your Brand Primary Color
Pick one color that best represents your answers above.
Use the psychology table above as your guide.

### Step 3 — Build Around It
- **Background:** 95–98% lightness version of your primary (or pure white/near-white)
- **Secondary:** Analogous color (30° away on color wheel) or same hue at different saturation
- **Accent:** Complementary color (180° away) — use sparingly

### Step 4 — Select Your CTA Color
Your CTA should be the highest-contrast color in the palette.
It must:
- Pass WCAG AA (4.5:1 contrast with its label text)
- Appear on no more than 2–3 elements per page
- Be noticeably different from your primary brand color

### Step 5 — Test Before Shipping
- Test with real users or run an A/B test on button color
- Check contrast ratios on WebAIM
- Preview in both light and dark mode
- Check on mobile — colors render differently on OLED vs. LCD screens
